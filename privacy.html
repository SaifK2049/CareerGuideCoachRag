Masari Private Beta Privacy Notice

This notice describes how the invitation-only Masari private beta handles the information you choose to submit. The beta operator is the person or organisation identified in your invitation. This notice must be reviewed with the operator’s legal identity and contact details before a wider public launch.

Information Masari processes

Masari stores your account email, career profile, CV text and uploaded PDF, target job paths, tracked job applications, knowledge evidence, saved AI analyses, action plans, job-specific CV guidance, interview practice questions, answers, completed-round feedback and practice progress, report-sharing settings, feedback, and limited product and operational events. Product events record app opens, section views, workflow outcomes, a random browser-session identifier, and the app version. Operational events record workflow outcomes, sanitized error codes, latency, AI model names, and token counts when available. These events do not contain CV text, job descriptions, application notes or contacts, evidence text, interview content, prompts, AI outputs, URLs, or error stack traces. If you join the waitlist, Masari stores the name and email address you submit so it can contact you about early access. It does not intentionally collect payment data during the free private beta.

Why it is processed

The information is used to provide your private workspace, retrieve relevant evidence, create cited career-gap analyses, understand feature adoption, improve workflow reliability, secure the service, investigate failures, export your data, and respond to beta feedback. Masari does not sell your career data or use it for advertising.

AI processing and service providers

When you request an analysis, CV guidance, interview question set, completed-round interview feedback, or a job-page import, relevant content you supplied or selected is sent server-side to an AI provider to create the requested result. Completed-round feedback sends the six saved answers and their questions. If a Premium user chooses microphone input, the recording is sent through Masari’s server function to the AI provider for transcription; Masari does not store the audio file, and the editable transcript is stored only after the user saves the answer. Imported job pages are retrieved only after you submit a supported HTTPS job-provider URL. Supabase provides authentication, database, private file storage, and server functions. Cloudflare provides web delivery and security. These providers process data only as needed to operate the beta under their applicable service terms.

Storage, access, and retention

Your workspace is separated by account-level database and storage rules. Other beta users cannot access it through the application. Authorized Masari administrators can view aggregate analytics, account identifiers and status metadata, and submitted beta feedback, but the admin analytics screen excludes CVs, job descriptions, application notes and contacts, evidence text, interview content, and AI-generated content. If you create a report link, anyone who has the unguessable link can view the selected path, analysis findings, and action-plan status until the link expires or you revoke it; CV text and private evidence details are excluded. CVs, career data, and successful analyses are retained until you clear the workspace or delete the account. Temporary processing records may be removed earlier. Product and operational events are automatically removed after 13 months.

Your controls

You can export a JSON copy of your account data, clear workspace content, or permanently delete the account from Profile & CV. The JSON export includes extracted CV text but does not embed the original PDF binary. Reply to your beta invitation for access, correction, deletion, privacy, or support requests.

Security and limitations

Masari uses authenticated access, row-level database controls, private file storage, rate limits, and server-side provider keys. No system can guarantee absolute security. Avoid submitting unnecessary special-category personal data, identity documents, references’ private contact details, or confidential employer information.
