Orynta Private Beta Terms

These terms apply to the free, invitation-only Orynta private beta. The beta operator is the person or organisation identified in your invitation. These terms are a practical beta draft and must be reviewed with the operator’s legal identity and jurisdiction before a public or paid launch.

Beta access

Access is personal, revocable, non-transferable, and provided only to invited testers. Do not share your account or attempt to access another user’s workspace. Orynta may change quotas, features, providers, or availability during the beta.

Your content

You retain responsibility for the CV, job descriptions, evidence, typed answers, microphone recordings, URLs, and feedback you submit. Only submit information you are entitled to use. Do not submit malware, unlawful content, confidential third-party data, or unnecessary sensitive personal information. You permit Orynta and its service providers to process submitted content solely to operate and improve the beta. Microphone use is optional; recordings are processed for transcription and are not retained by Orynta.

AI output

Orynta provides automated career analysis and interview preparation for informational purposes. AI, transcription, and extraction systems can be incomplete or wrong. Interview scores assess the submitted answer content only; they are not hiring probabilities and do not assess accent, voice, personality, protected traits, or employability. Citations and source labels show which submitted passages influenced a result; they do not make the result professional employment, legal, financial, immigration, or certification advice. Verify important decisions independently.

Availability and changes

The beta is experimental and provided without an availability commitment. Features may fail, data formats may change, and the service may be suspended for security, maintenance, abuse, or beta conclusion. Export important data regularly.

Acceptable use

Do not probe security controls, overwhelm the service, automate account access without permission, reverse engineer restricted components, use the beta to make decisions about other people, or upload content that infringes rights or law.

Ending participation

You can end participation by deleting your account. The operator may end access for misuse, risk, inactivity, or beta closure. Account deletion is designed to remove private application data and stored CV files, subject to short-lived provider backups and legal obligations.

Feedback

You may provide product feedback. Do not include CV or job-description text in the feedback field. The operator may use feedback to improve Orynta without identifying you publicly.
